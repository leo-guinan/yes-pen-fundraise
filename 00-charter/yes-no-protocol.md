# Yes/No Investor Protocol

## The Binary Framework

**The Yes Pen Raise operates on a simple binary:**

- ✅ **YES** — "I believe what you're building and I'm in."
- ❌ **NO** — "This isn't for me."

No questions. No endless calls. No dragging founders into validation theater. No pretend interest. No "Let's stay in touch."

## The Investor Filter Statement

Use this everywhere:

> "We run a Yes/No raise. Diligence is crowdsourced, transparent, and always-on. You can read everything in the Yes Room. If you see it, invest. If you don't see it, that's okay — this raise isn't for you."

## The Script

**For investor conversations:**

> "I run a Yes/No raise. Everything you need is in the Yes Room — the crowdsourced diligence, the data, the diagrams, and the evaluations from scouts. I don't do repeated meetings or back-and-forth. You can make a decision in 20 minutes: Yes or No. If you see the future I'm building, I'd love to have you. If you don't, no harm — this isn't for you."

## Why This Works

### 1. Reverses the Power Dynamic

Most founders chase investors. This tells investors:

- I don't chase
- I don't pitch
- I don't audition
- I don't let you waste my time
- I don't explain myself beyond the public materials
- I don't do diligence theater

This filters out 80% of industry noise.

### 2. Signals Extreme Confidence

Only founders who know their thesis holds up can do this.

You're telling them:

> "I've done more diligence on myself than you could. My scouts have done more diligence than your analysts. My community has surfaced more signal than your partners. You can either trust the signal or walk away."

### 3. Attracts the Right Investors

The ones you want are:

- Fast
- Decisive
- Intuitive
- Sovereign
- High-context
- Non-paternalistic
- Partnership-oriented

These people LOVE binary decisions. They want no wasting time, no adversarial vetting, no power games, no "prove it to me, kid."

### 4. Aligns with Conscious Economics

You are modeling:

- Low verification distance
- Low friction decision-making
- High-context communication
- No time violence
- Mutual sovereignty
